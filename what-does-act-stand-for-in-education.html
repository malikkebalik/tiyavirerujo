ACT is an acronym meaning “American College Test”. The ACT is a standardized test to determine a high school graduate’s preparation for college-level work. Scholastic Aptitude Test Similarly, what is the difference between the ACT and the SAT? Scoring Differences The SAT is scored on a scale of 600 to 2400, with a score of 200 to 800 possible on each of the three sections. The ACT is scored with a composite of one to 36 based on average scores from the four test sections, each of which is also scored from one to 36.Is a 17 on the ACT good?A score of 17 is pretty low. It places you in the bottom 31st percentile nationally out of the 2 million students taking the ACT entrance exam this year. For comparison purposes, a 17 on the ACT converts to a 930 on the SAT based on the College Board / ACT concordance.
