Techland Will Continue To Support Dying Light Throughout 2016

 Posted on July 14, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  243 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Recommended Videos

Fresh off launching undead expansion The Following, developer Techland has committed to supporting Dying Light throughout the course of 2016, with new content still to be announced.

More than a year after the release of the base game, Dying Light: The Following was met with rave reviews, proving that there’s still a demand for the studio’s parkour zombie title in 2016. Here, Techland CEO Paweł Marchewka thanked players for their continued support, all the while teasing at the new DLC still to come for the game.

“We always had faith in the project, but seeing so many enthusiastic opinions among players and high review scores from the press is utterly heart-warming,” wrote Marchewka. “At the same time it is a clear sign that there is still high demand for that unique Dying Light experience. To make sure that every new or veteran player gets all the fun they deserve, we have decided to continue our support for Dying Light at least till the end of this year. We are already working on new unannounced content, so stay tuned for our future news.”

Upholding an open dialog with its userbase, Marchewka went on to encourage fans to submit their own ideas for the future of Dying Light, be it in the form of new areas to explore or advanced weaponry.

The Following, meanwhile, represents the next step for the budding new franchise, and you can check out our verdict on Techland’s expansion in the meantime.
