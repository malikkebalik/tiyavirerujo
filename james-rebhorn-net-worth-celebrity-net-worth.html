    Early Life

    James Rebhorn was born James Robert Rebhorn on September 1, 1948, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. His father, James, was a salesman and industrial engineer, and his mother, Ardell, "loved him very much and supported all his dreams" (according to Rebhorn's obituary). James had a sister named Janice, and the family moved to Anderson, Indiana, during his childhood. Rebhorn attended Madison Heights High School, graduating in 1966, then he moved to Springfield, Ohio, to study political science at Wittenberg University. As a college student, he joined the Lambda Chi Alpha fraternity and appeared in the plays "Lysistrata" and "Tricks of Scapin." James graduated in 1970, then he enrolled at Columbia University's School of the Arts in New York City. After earning his Master of Fine Arts in acting in 1972, he got involved in the metropolitan theater scene.

    Death

    James passed away at his New Jersey home on March 21, 2014, at the age of 65 while under hospice care for melanoma. He had been battling melanoma for more than two decades. Rebhorn wrote his obituary, which read, in part, "Jim was fortunate enough to earn his living doing what he loved. He was a professional actor.
